Summary

In order to test the functionality of our website we asked our testers to complete four simple tasks and to fill out a Google Form with any feedback they may have. Our tasks were:

  • Task 1 - Find out how much the restaurants' panna cotta costs.
  • Task 2 - Find out what the restaurants' phone number is.
  • Task 3 - Find the restaurants' address.
  • Task 4 - Place an order. (We informed the testers that no actual order would be placed)

Most of our testers had no trouble finishing the four tasks. We will discuss the details further down in this webpage.


Link to our Google Form

Tester audience

We tested the website with users mostly from the target age group, although there are some testers outside of that age range. The percentage of age groups of our tests were as follows: 80% were aged between 20-25 years, 10% aged 15 and 10% aged 50. This fits with the target audience, as the majority of the test group are young adults. At the same time, the wide range of ages polled allows us to be more confident that most people, regardless of age, would have little trouble navigating our website. During the testing process we observed some of the testers and noted any potential feedback. The feedback will be discussed further down this webpage. As for the observations we made, the testers seemingly preferred to use the footer and header to navigate through the webpage, although a few used the main page to navigate. Task one was usually achieved very quickly, around 5-10 seconds, although we had one tester fail to find the panna-cotta at which point we told them to go to the next task. Task two was the task most testers finished the quickest, taking approximately 2-8 seconds to achieve on average. Most testers scrolled to the footer in order to find the phone number, although a few chose to go to the contact-us page instead, hence the variation in times. Task three had similar results to task two, although some testers were confused by the map on our order-now page since our restaurant is not a real location. It took the testers approximately 5 - 15 seconds to find the address. As we anticipated task four took the longest, our testers had no issues navigating the website and submitting a fake order. This task took approximately 30s - 1.5 minutes to finish on average.

Testing platforms

60% of the testers viewed the website on mobile, 30% viewed it on computer and the remaining 10% viewed it on Mac. Mobile tester also reported the website being difficult to use. This makes sense as the webpage is designed to be viewed on desktop/laptop. Mobile compatibility is something we could add in the future. Furthermore 40% of our testers viewed our webpage through Chrome, 20% viewed it through Firefox, 20% viewed it through Snapchat, 10% viewed it through Edge, and 10% viewed it through Opera. This gives us a large variation in different browsers used, which further helps us understand the strengths and weaknesses of our website, although the biggest issue was clearly the lack of mobile compatibility.

Feedback summary

Overall feedback on the web page's design was positive. A lot of the testers complained that there was no popup after placing an order. Such a popup would redirect the user to a third-party website to sort the payment, which was beyond the scope of this assignment. In hindsight, we could have added a small window popup which displays "order placed" as a confirmation. Another highly requested feature was to make the Papa's logo re-direct the user to the main page. This thought did not cross our minds while making the website, although we definitely agree that such a feature should be implemented. Some testers requested the website to be more dynamic. This was especially requested by mobile phone users as the website was not designed for mobile phones in mind. Other testers requested a function which would allow the user to reserve a table. We agree that such a function would be a nice addition to our webpage. Others did not like specific design choices, ranging from disliking the color-scheme to disliking the sectioning of the website. This is something we consider as we continue to develop the webpage. Feedback on how easy our webpage is to navigate was mostly positive, although the mobile phone users had some trouble navigating through our website. This is also taken into account.

Our response

As we mentioned throughout this document our number one priority would be to implement mobile compatibility. Most people nowadays use their phones to surf the web, it is therefore important to add mobile functionality to the webpage so we can reach as big of an audience as possible. Furthermore, we would focus on making our webpage more dynamic in general. Another functionality we would like to add would be the possibility to press the Papa's logo to go to the main page. A popup window confirming the order would also be a nice addition. Additionally, we could possibly add multiple languages to our website. Lastly, we would want to add a feature allowing the user to reserve a table.

Self-reflection

Seeing as this was a group project, we found it to be an incredibly academically enriching experience. Planning how to distribute tasks amongst the group members, working together, and finalizing the assignment helped us experience how it is to work in a group of likeminded individuals with a shared goal. We believe that this experience will be incredibly useful to us when we start working in companies after we are done with university. Additionally, working together allowed us to help each other better understand HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and web-design in general. Furthermore, working on a full website was really motivating and we are proud of our final product.
